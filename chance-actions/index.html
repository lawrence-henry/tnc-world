    Chapter 5: Chance Actions¶

    Chance Actions cover a wide array of potential maneuvers a character can attempt, whether in or out of combat. They are fueled from a pool of Chance Points, built up by rolling Doubles and Triples on the dice.

    Doubles and Triples

    • Chance Points gained by rolling Doubles is equal to the value of the Chance Die.

    • Chance Points gained by rolling Triples is equal to the value of the Chance Die +6.

    GM Tip - Chance Pool

    At your discretion, players begin every Game Session with 6 Chance Points in their pool, ready to be used.

    Unless specifically stated otherwise, the vast majority of Chance Actions are used in conjunction with a Major Action (such as an Attack, Grapple, or Action Roll).

    Detailed below are descriptions, rules, and examples of the Chance Actions you can potentially perform during a game session. These Chance Actions are offered as a way to make Action Rolls more dynamic.

    Chance Actions are not bound by type.

    Note: While the Chance Actions are separated into multiple lists, they are not mutually exclusive. If you can justify a Non-Combat Chance Action in a combat situation, then you can spend the CP required to perform the action. Likewise, if you can somehow justify using a Combat Chance Action in a less than hostile situation, then you can spend the CP required to perform it.

    General Combat¶

    Assessment (1+ CP): Choose a single target. As a Free Action, make a Perception (Empathy) Action Roll CR 10 + your target's Wits (Deception). If you win, ignore up to 5 points of your Target's total Defense. Add one additional target per CP spent.
    Acrobatic Bluff (1+ CP): Gain up to a +3 bonus to your Defense by spending a Minor Action and rolling a Reflexes (Acrobatics) Action Roll CR 10 + your target's Perception (Empathy).
    Brace (1+ CP): When subjected to a Saving Throw, you can spend an Instinct Action to increase your roll against that Saving Throw by 1 per CP spent.
    Team Attack (2 CP): On a successful attack, choose an ally that can see and hear you within 6 yards. That ally can use an Instinct Action to immediately attack the same enemy, with a +1 bonus.
    Taunt (2 CP): Roll a Willpower (Deception) Action Roll as a Minor Action versus Perception (Empathy) or Willpower (Self-Discipline) of any target within 10 yards. If you win, any aggressive actions made by that opponent must target you for the next minute (4 rounds).
    Back-to-Back (2 CP): You bolster an adjacent ally's defense, providing both of you with a +1 bonus to Defense until the start of your next turn.
    Savage Striker (2 CP): Your attack does an extra +2 Damage.
    Precision Attack (2 CP): Your attack ignores up to 2 AP provided by equipment.
    Evasive Maneuvers (3 CP): As an Instinct Action, you can impose a -2 penalty on an opponent's Ranged Attack Roll.[evaluation required]
    Rapid Attack (3 CP): You can make one additional attack, either against an enemy you are already engaged with, or a different target within range and sight. You must have a loaded Ranged weapon to make a Ranged Attack. Doubles on this attack do not produce more CP.
    Inflict Wounds (3 CP): Your attack deals 1/2 its Base Damage but attempts to inflict a single Tier 1 Condition, based on damage type (i.e.: Bleeding, Winded, Weakened). The target must roll a Constitution Saving Throw CR 8 + your Chance Die or suffer the Condition.
    Heavy Hitter (6 CP): Your attack ignores up to 5 AP provided by Equipment.
    Shocking Brutality (6 CP): If you take out an opponent, you can spend your Instinct Action. Any opponents within 6 yards to witness the attack must roll a Willpower Saving Throw CR 10 + your Chance Die from the attack roll. On a successful Save, the targets gain Tier 1 of the Fear Condition. On a failed Save, Tier 3.
    Lethal Blow (6 CP): Your attack does an extra +5 Damage.
    Close Call (9 CP): If an attack would take you to 0 Health, you fall Unconscious with 1 Health instead.
    Last Stand (9 CP): After falling to 0 Health, you can activate this Chance Action as an Instinct Action. Doing so gives you a single round where you ignore all Damage and Conditions. During this round, no Healing Action Rolls work to your benefit, and you fall to Dying with 1d6 rounds removed from the Dying timer.

    Melee¶

    Footwork (1+ CP): On a successful hit, move yourself or your opponent up to 2 yards per CP spent in any direction. Upward movement is halved.
    Parry (1+ CP): As an Instinct Action, you make an opposed Melee Attack Roll against your opponent. If you win, your next Melee Attack against that opponent gains a bonus equal to the CP spent into this Action.
    Bullying Blow (2 CP): On a successful hit, you can attempt to inflict the Fear Condition on an opponent as a Free Action. Your opponent must make a Willpower Saving Throw CR 10 + your Chance Die, gaining the next tier of the Condition on a failure.
    Disarm (2 CP): You attempt to disarm the target with your melee attack. You and your opponent must make an opposed attack roll. These attack rolls do not generate Chance Points. If you win the test, you knock your enemy’s weapon a number of yards away equal to your Chance Die result + Might.
    Trip (2 CP): Instead of dealing damage, make an opposed Might (Strength) test. If you win, your Melee Attack knocks your enemy prone.
    Iron Fist (3 CP): Ignore up to 2 AP from natural sources. This does not ignore AP provided by Armor.
    Bind Up (3 CP): As an Instinct Action, you make an opposed Melee Attack Roll against your opponent. If you win, you may immediately make an Unarmed Attack against them.
    Riposte (3 CP): If an opponent misses you with a Melee Attack before the start of your next turn, you can make an immediate Melee Counterattack against them as an Instinct Action, adding your Wits to the Damage Total.
    Vicious Stomp (6 CP): Make an immediate attack against a target that falls prone within your reach as an Instinct Action. If you hit, add your Chance Die to the Damage (this stacks with the bonuses from rolling Doubles and Triples).
    Whirlwind Assault (6 CP): Make a series of attacks against all enemies within range of your Melee Attack. Make a single Attack Action Roll, comparing it to each enemy's Defense, resolving Damage as normal.
    Devastating Combination (6 CP): You make additional Melee Attacks in rapid succession. Make a single Attack Action Roll with a +2 bonus to the roll. You land a number of hits equal to the value on your Chance Die, adding that amount to your Damage Total.

    Ranged¶

    Covering Fire (1+ CP): When an opponent targets any creature other than you, make a Ranged Attack Action Roll against it as an Instinct Action. If you miss, that opponent suffers a Minor Disadvantage (-2) on their Attack Action Roll. If you hit, that opponent suffers a Major Disadvantage (-5) on their Attack Action Roll.
    Stock Striker (1 CP): Make a Melee Attack with your Firearm. Gain a +1 bonus to the Attack Action Roll when you use a two-handed firearm. On a success, your target must make a Might Saving Throw CR 10 + your Chance Die or fall Prone and Stunned.
    Strafe (2 CP): While using a firearm with the Autofire property, you target a 10 yard cone. Opponents in the area up to your weapon's Long Range must make a Reflexes Saving Throw CR 10 + your Perception Ability, taking your weapon's normal Damage on a failure.
    Overwatch (3 CP): Designate a target behind full cover that you have already perceived. If that target moves before the start of your next turn, Roll a Ranged Attack Action Roll as an Instinct Action.
    Tactical Takedown (6 CP): Whenever a target falls Prone within your CQB or Short Range, you can make a single Ranged Attack against that target as an Instinct Action. On a successful hit, add twice your Chance Die to the Damage (this stacks with the bonuses from rolling Doubles and Triples).
    One Shot Left (12 CP): As long as you have only one piece of ammunition loaded in your weapon and on your person, you can spend an Instinct Action. If you do, gain a +5 bonus to your Base Damage, and add 3x your Chance Die to the Damage (this stacks with the bonuses from rolling Doubles and Triples).
    Headshot (18 CP): You spend an Instinct Action, a Free Action, and a Minor Action, to line up a devastating shot. On a successful hit, deal Critical Damage x2.

    Grappling¶

    Shoulder Toss (1+ CP): Roll a Grapple Action Roll. If you succeed, you throw the target Prone in any space you threaten, or up to 1 yard away per CP spent. This ends the Grapple.
    Catch Attack (2 CP): Roll an opposed Attack Action Roll against Melee or Thrown Attacks as an Instinct Action. If you succeed against a Melee Attack, your opponent is Grappled. If you succeed against a Thrown Attack, you can choose to have the weapon in your hand, ready for use.
    Desperate Grab (3 CP): On any attack (yours or your opponent's) that ends with a Near-Miss, you can spend an Instinct Action to initiate a Grapple.
    Binding Throw (3 CP): On a successful Throw Action Roll, you initiate a Lock Action Roll as an Instinct Action. Your target makes a Reflexes Saving Throw CR 10 + your Chance Die.
    Deadly Grapple (6 CP): While you are in control of a Grapple, you can make an Attack Action Roll as a Minor Action with a +3 bonus to your Base Damage. This attack ends the Grapple.
    Bone Breaker (12 CP): While you are in control of a Grapple, you initiate a Lock Action Roll with a Minor Disadvantage (-2). Your target makes a Reflexes or Might Saving Throw (target's choice) CR 10 + your Chance Die. On a successful Save, the Target suffers the Weakness (T2) Condition until they are able to take a Rest. On a failed save, the target suffers Weakness (T4) until they are able to get Surgery.
    Chokehold (12 CP): While you are in control of a Grapple, you can initiate a Chokehold as a Major Action. Your target makes a Constitution Saving Throw CR 10 + your Might Ability. On a failed save, the target gains Fatigue (T1). The target makes the Saving Throw every round you maintain the Chokehold with a Major Action. On a successful save of 2 or more, they break free of the Grapple.
    Sickle and Hammer (12 CP): Make an opposed Attack Roll against an incoming Melee Attack. If you succeed, your target's attack fails and you initiate a Grapple, dealing Unarmed Critical Damage.
    Neckbreaker (18 CP): While you are in control of a Grapple, you initiate a Lock Action Roll as a Major Action. Your target makes a Constitution Saving Throw CR 10 + your Chance Die. On a successful Save, the target suffers the Incapacitated Condition until they are able to get Surgery. On a failed Save, the target suffers the Paralyzed Condition and begins Suffocating.
