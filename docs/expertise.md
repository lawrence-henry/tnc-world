# Chapter 4: Particulars of Expertise {#expertise}

## Traits

<!-- prettier-ignore -->
!!! abstract "What a Trait Represents"
    A Trait represents **focused training or experience**, not raw talent. As such:

    - Traits do **not** grant new actions unless explicitly stated.
    - Traits provide a **situational bonus** to Action Rolls where applicable.
    - Traits may be referenced by other rules (Expertise, Chance Actions, Equipment), but are not locked to them (if another Trait could apply, speak to your GM).
    - Traits never stack with other Traits on the same Action Roll.

---

A Trait is a focused area of proficiency within its related ability. A Trait placed in a Primary Ability grants a +3 bonus when making an Action Roll related to it. This bonus is only a +2 for Secondary Ability Traits. The GM will usually let you know what Trait applies to each test, but if it isn't clear don't be afraid to ask if a Trait applies.

Starting at Level 11, you may select a Trait you already have, to increase your bonus with it by 1. This increases Primary Ability Traits to a +4, and Secondary Ability Traits to a +3.

Variable Traits are those that don't fit cleanly into any single category. When you take a Trait in the Variable list, you can apply it to one of your Ability Scores. You must be able to justify your use of a Trait should your choice be questionable. Be sure to discuss these with your GM to determine.

<!-- prettier-ignore -->
??? info "Creating Unique Traits"
    If a GM or Player can justify the creation of a Trait, feel free to do so. GM's, especially, should do this for high-profile NPCs and elite enemies.

    The primary caveat, of course, is to be sure the scope of your Trait is important enough to give you an edge, but limited in scope to keep the game fair.

### Working in Multiple Disciplines

<!-- prettier-ignore -->
!!! summary "Cross-Training at a Glance"
    - Gain the benefits of a second Background
    - Recieve:
        - 1 Weapon Group (if available)
        - 1 Background Benefit
        - +10 RP Income
    - Can be taken twice (with expanded options at Level 11+)

Some people career hop. Some go into dual-degree programs. There isn't a one-size-fits-all categorization of background experiences. Since the backgrounds in TNC function effectively like classes in other systems, and being trained for various applications of skill is a real-life experience, these Traits operate outside of the normal Trait rules. Like Variable Traits, these can potentially fit under multiple Abilities, and can therefore be listed under any of them.

Instead of the normal bonus gained by taking a Trait, these options allow you to pick up an additional Background, and grant you the following from the chosen secondary Background: 1 Weapon Group Training (if available), 1 chosen Background Benefit, and their monthly RP earnings increases by +10 for leveling and monthly income purchases. If taken a second time at or beyond level 11, the PC can acquire 1 additional Background training, using the above rules, which stack with the first. Alternatively, they can gain 1 additional Background benefit from their first cross-training, and their RP earnings increase by another (for a maximum of +20). This does not affect Mission income.

## Expertises

Expertise is what separates the commanders from their rank-and-file counterparts. As characters grow in their abilities, it's likely they'll specialize in mastering their disciplines. Prerequisite training required to begin training in an expertise is indicated in its block.

Each Expertise comes in four tiers, which must be taken in order: **Basic**, which covers the bare minimum of training required to use an Expertise's abilities. **Initiate**, which indicates a moderate level of training. **Advanced**, which is the point at which mastery begins to truly show. And, finally, **Expert**, which indicates true, full mastery in a given Expertise.

The Expertises listed are separated into categories, indicating how they would most likely be used.
