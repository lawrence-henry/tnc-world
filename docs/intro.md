# TNC World

A compact, narrative-focused tabletop roleplaying game about exploration, conflict, and small groups forced to make hard choices in a changing world.

## Overview
TNC World is a rules-light system that centers on character motives, player-driven scenes, and fast resolution. Emphasis is on story beats and consequences rather than simulation: tense choices, risky plans, and the social costs of survival.

## What you need
- 1 Gamemaster (GM) and 2–6 players  
- 1–2 six-sided dice (d6) per player (or a dice pool app)  
- Character sheets (one per player)  
- Tokens or markers for resource tracking  

## Core themes
- Survival vs. Ethics: tradeoffs between doing what's needed and who you become doing it  
- Small-group dynamics: leadership, trust, and betrayal under pressure  
- Mystery of the world: fragments to discover, consequences to unwrap

## How to play (quick)
1. Create characters: pick a role, a driving desire, and one secret.  
2. The GM frames a scene and prompts player actions.  
3. Players declare an action and roll d6 (simple success/failure or use a small pool).  
4. GM describes results and advances threats or rewards.  
5. Track resources and complications; use short downtime between scenes to develop characters.

## Safety and session zero
- Run a short session zero to set tone, boundaries, and consent.  
- Use safety tools (X-card, lines & veils) and establish hard limits.  
- Agree on the level of violence, horror, and mature themes beforehand.

## For the GM
- Start small: one clear objective and one strong antagonist.  
- Keep scenes short and move the spotlight between players.  
- Use complications to force meaningful choices rather than arbitrary penalties.

## Next steps
- Read the rules reference for character generation, mechanics, and combat.  
- Try a one-shot scenario to learn pacing and flow.  
- Share feedback and house rules with your table.

## The Nomad Initiative
Service in honor. Hope in Brotherhood. Victory in Sacrifice. This is what it means to be a Nomad. The Nomad Initiative began as an experimental military unit comprised of otherwise ordinary people intentionally infected with what is known as the Nano-Virus.

The Nano-Virus enhances the human body beyond its normal limits. It provides some with additional strength, resilience, or speed. As such, it turned its initial units into powerful weapons of war. But, as with all experiments, there were side effects. Some that were infected failed to assimilate the virus correctly, becoming grotesque and dangerous creatures.

The program was eventually scrapped by the military, but its creator continued in secret, perfecting his creation. 
Survivors of that initial program have become parents. And, unknown to those in command, many of their progeny carry a successful iteration of the virus within their blood.

## What to Expect
The Nomad Chronicles system assumes a modern level of technology, with a little dabble into future tech. Power armor exists but is extremely experimental. Only a few suits exist, and they're jealously guarded. Handheld frictionless railgun weapons as well.

For the most part, however, whatever you can find in the modern world, you should have access to in this system. The core of the Nomad Chronicles is an exploration of survival, resilience, friendship, and struggling with your inner demons. Players and Game Masters (GMs) are encouraged to explore these themes in their games. Though, as with all systems; rules, themes, and other such situations, are guidelines secondary to your enjoyment of the game.

Given the history behind the Nomad Initiative and the Nano-Virus, quite a few kinds of stories are possible using this system. Perhaps the players are recruits in a black-ops unit tasked with overthrowing a nation. Perhaps they're college friends starting their own mercenary outfit. Or, as in the initial Nomad novel, they could even be attempting to survive in the event of a large-scale failed infection.

In all cases, it's likely the players will need to emphasize teamwork, strategy, and creative problem-solving. At times, it will take that and more to succeed in the mission.

## The Structure
The mechanics for this system are loosely based on the Adventure Game Engine (AGE) 3d6 ruleset. Let's take a moment or two to address how this system differs, is structured, and how to use it. Because, as we all know, Tabletop Role-Playing Games (TTRPGs) are more about the application of knowledge, rather than simply having a lot of knowledge.

**System Foundations:** The Nomad Chronicles (TNC) draws inspiration from the 3d6-based resolution mechanics popularized by Green Ronin Publishing's Adventure Game Engine (AGE). TNC diverges substantially, however, in mechanics, execution, and structure.
- **Ability Scores:** TNC uses six core abilities with a unique range and scaling, as opposed to AGE's nine core abilities.
- **Action Resolution:** While both systems use 3d6, TNC introduces the Chance Die, that feeds from and interacts with a persistent Chance Pool for tactical ability use rather than triggering immediate-use Chance Actions.
- **Progression:** TNC features Experience Points and Survivor Points for advancement, allowing granular character growth through ability improvements, Traits, gear proficiencies, infection level, and mutations.
- **Traits &amp; Expertise:** TNC separates **Traits** (small bonuses) from deep, multi-tiered **Expertise**, creating a hybrid between class features and skill trees.
- **Combat:** :: TNC emphasizes cinematic, condition-based combat with custom mechanics for cover, knockback, mutation states, and tactical positioning.
- **Thematic Setting:** While the concept of super soldiers is nothing new to fiction, the viral nature of the Nomads, alongside item degradation, Requisition Points (RP), and narrative "Virus Mode" moments, provides a wholly distinct flavor and gameplay experience.test
