Sometimes, a fight spills farther than you may have anticipated. Sometimes, a character needs to get in or out of a situation in a hurry. This subchapter includes vehicle rules, statistics, and modifications for resolving actions where vehicles are involved. That said, vehicles can be damaged. See [**Equipment: Damage & Destruction, pg. 85**](#85) subchapter for more information.

###### Maneuvering

Every vehicle has a Maneuver Modifier, indicating how much some vehicles are easier to maneuver than others. Additionally, every vehicle has a Top Speed, indicated on the chart below. There are times when two vehicles attempt to outmaneuver one another, but one happens to be significantly faster or more nimble.

- **Penalty for Speed:** Each speed category above **Street Speed** imposes a cumulative -1 penalty to Maneuver checks. Each category below **Street Speed** grants a cumulative +1 bonus.
- **Action Types:**
  - **Minor Action:** Simple maneuvers (e.g., driving straight, gentle turns).
  - **Major Action:** Complex maneuvers (e.g., sharp turns, drifting, evasive actions).

**Maneuver Action Rolls:** Reflexes + relevant skill (Riding, Driving, Piloting) + Maneuver Score +/&minus; Speed Penalty

##### Speed Categories

| Speed Category | Speed Range (mph)             | Speed (yards/round) | Collision Damage |
| :------------- | ----------------------------- | ------------------- | ---------------- |
| Stationary     | 0                             | 0                   | 0                |
| Alley Speed    | 1-25 mph                      | 12                  | 2                |
| Street Speed   | 26-50 mph                     | 25                  | 3                |
| Highway Speed  | 51-70 mph                     | 32                  | 4                |
| High Speed     | 71+ mph                       | 36+                 | 5                |
| Reverse Speed  | One category below Top Speed. | -                   | 1                |

Drivers can use a minor action to change speed categories, shifting up or down by 1 category at a time. Only certain modifications can increase a vehicle's Speed Category beyond High Speed.

Any Air Vehicle that cannot effectively hover, must maintain Highway or High Speed. At Street Speed, the aircraft begins descending. At Alley Speed or below, the aircraft stalls, dropping into a freefall that requires a CR18 Piloting (Reflexes) test to recover from without crashing the vehicle.

##### Races, Chases, & Crashes

You've made a hasty retreat, but your enemy's hot on your tail, firing rifles at you. Diving into vehicles, you and your enemy drive off, chasing and fighting through the city streets.

Below are rules regarding, as the subchapter title has indicated, the racing, chasing, and crashing of vehicles. Under normal circumstances, these rules and mechanics aren't necessary. These are for the times that are out of the ordinary, when bullets are flying and adrenaline is the only reason you're ahead. We'll start with what happens when a Chase Encounter begins. Like any other encounter, the GM will call for an Initiative roll.

**1. Determine Starting Speed Category:**
: Most of the time, this will be Stationary, but occasionally, you'll be out driving when a chase begins, at which point you'll need to know your current Speed Category.

**2. Chase Start Positions:**
: The lead vehicle sets the pace, while others attempt to follow or overtake. These encounters begin with 1 point of relative distance between the two vehicles.

**3. Roll Initiative:**
: Roll initiatives to determine the order of actions as usual.

**4. Drivers:**
: For the most part, the Driver will be focused on using Maneuvers to evade, catch, or overtake another vehicle. Should they need to perform actions other than driving, unless they're traveling Alley Speed, both their Maneuver and other attempted test are made with a Minor Disadvantage at Street Speed, or a Major Disadvantage at Highway Speed and above.

**5. Passengers:**
: A passenger in a vehicle can perform just about any test they normally would be able to, within reason. This can include making ranged attacks, throwing explosives, or what have you. Action Rolls made by passengers are made with a Minor Disadvantage at Highway Speed, or a Major Disadvantage at High Speed and above.

**6. Maneuvers:**
: Maneuvers are the bread and butter of the drivers. Maneuvers come in two categories: Simple, and Complex. These categories, for simplicity's sake, can also be labeled Minor Actions and Major Actions, respectively. Sample maneuvers have been detailed below. As with other open-ended rules like this, for more specific maneuvers, consult with your GM for their classification and Maneuver CR. If there's a really cool Maneuver you think is missing, check the Vehicle-Specific Chance Actions chart for it first.

**7. Damage:**
: Some maneuvers could result in damage to your vehicle. And, potentially, to your vehicle's occupants. Any collision deals the Collision Damage as indicated in the chart.

    Minor Collisions can happen at Street Speed or below, usually as a result of failing a Simple Maneuver. Major Collisions can happen at Street Speed or higher, usually as a result of failing a Complex Maneuver. In the event of any collision, the damage is determined by the speed of the vehicle at the time of impact.

    A Minor Collision force-shifts the vehicle down by one Speed Category, and deals damage as normal. A Major Collision force-shifts the vehicle down by two Speed Categories, and adds its AP to the Collision Damage. Any damage that exceeds the AP of the vehicle is transferred to its occupants and remains unmodified.

    Finally, collision damage is unique, in that the Collision Damage is multiplied by the Chance Die to determine the full damage.

!!! example "Example Collisions"
    - Ex. 1
    - Vehicle: Sports Car (AP 5)
    - Collision - Street Speed. Driver uses Floor It and fails their Maneuver Action Roll, sideswiping another vehicle.
    - Collision Damage: 3
    - Chance Die: 3
    - Total Collision Damage: 9
    - Damage to Vehicle: 9 - 5 = 4
    - Excess Damage Transferred to Character: 4

    - Ex. 2
    - Vehicle: Sports Car (AP 5)
    - Collision - Highway Speed. Driver uses Slipping and fails their Maneuver Action Roll, unintentionally running into the back of another vehicle.
    - Collision Damage: 4
    - Chance Die: 3
    - Total Collision Damage: 27
    - Damage to Vehicle: 27 - 5 = 22
    - Excess Damage Transferred to Character: 22

Note that the above examples assume an average roll on the Chance Die. The CD multiplication is intended, as even the most seemingly benign vehicle crash could potentially result in severe or fatal injury.

**8. Escape:**
: If, at any point, the two vehicles end up ten or more relative distance points apart, or if line of sight is broken for 1 minute (4 rounds) someone in the following vehicle must make a Perception Action Roll CR 18. If successful, they spot the lead vehicle and resume the chase at 7 distance points. Failure means the lead vehicle has escaped, regardless of collisions, ending the encounter.

**9. Vehicle-Specific Chance Actions:**
: Whenever a character in a vehicle rolls doubles, they may use one of the Vehicle-Specific Chance Actions as indicated in the chart below.

##### Simple Maneuvers (Minor Actions)

**Accelerate:**
: Increase speed by one category.

**Brake:**
: Decrease speed by one category.

**Turn:**
: Change the vehicle's direction by up to 90 degrees, decreasing speed by two categories. Maneuver CR 12.

**Slalom:**
: Avoid oncoming obstacles, decreasing speed by one category. Maneuver CR 12.

**Floor It:**
: Increase speed by two categories. Maneuver CR 12.

**Mirror Check:**
: Gain a Minor Advantage to your next Maneuver Action Roll.

**Deploy:**
: If you have traps or weapons that do not require aiming, you deploy them onto the roadway, causing a surprise obstacle and imposing a Minor Disadvantage on the following vehicle's Maneuver Action Rolls until the end of the round.

##### Complex Maneuvers (Major Actions)

**Sharp Turn:**
: Change the vehicle's direction by up to 180&deg; without losing speed. Maneuver CR 15.

**Power Slide:**
: Slide the vehicle through up to 90&deg;, decreasing speed by one category. Maneuver CR 17.

**Weave Through Traffic:**
: Move through dense traffic without decreasing speed. Maneuver CR 17.

**Ram:**
: Attempt to ram another vehicle. Both vehicles take damage. If both vehicles are traveling at the same speed category, do not multiply the damage roll. Maneuver Action Roll CR 15.

**Jump:**
: Attempt to launch your vehicle over an obstacle or gap. Maneuver Action Roll CR 15.

**Slipping:**
: Attempt to use another vehicle's slipstream to keep up with it. Success allows you to increase speed to your target's speed category. Maneuver Action Roll CR 14.

**Nitro Boost:**
: Temporarily increase speed by up to two categories for one minute. Maneuver CR 12.

##### Vehicle-Specific Chance Actions (3CP)

**Evasive Maneuver:**
: You focus on defending your vehicle from incoming damage, granting your character's Defense a +2 bonus, and a +2 bonus to Maneuver Action Rolls until the end of your next turn.

**Pit Maneuver:**
: A focused Ram attack that forces the target vehicle to lose control and spin, forcing the target to make a Maneuver Action Roll CR 18, or collide with a nearby obstacle, ignoring 4 points of their vehicle's AP in determining how much damage the vehicle can absorb.

**Side Swipe:**
: A ram maneuver in which you attempt to force the target vehicle off the road, directly into an oncoming obstacle. The target must make an immediate Maneuver Action Roll CR 15 or suffer a collision.

**Reverse 180:**
: A rapid 180&deg; turn starting from reverse, granting any passengers a +3 bonus on Ranged Attack rolls against a target until the end of the current round.

**J-Turn:**
: A rapid 180&deg; turn while moving forward. Can be used for escapes, or grants any passengers a +2 bonus on Ranged Attack rolls against a target.

**Double Clutch:**
: Gain a +1 bonus to Top Speed until the beginning of your next turn.

**Brake Check:**
: Rapidly decrease your speed by 2 categories, potentially allowing the following vehicle to catch up or overtake the vehicle. Can be used to force a Ram maneuver, or grant any passengers a +5 bonus on ranged attack rolls until the beginning of your next turn.

**Emergency Brake:**
: Stop the vehicle immediately, reducing Speed Category to Stationary. Can be used after breaking line of sight to avoid detection. Imposes a Minor Disadvantage to enemy Perception tests.

**Burnout:**
: Spin the tires from Stationary or Alley Speed, creating a smoke screen, imposing a Minor Disadvantage to enemy Maneuver Action Rolls until the beginning of your next turn.

**Half-Wheeling:**
: Balance the vehicle on less than the optimal number of available wheels to navigate tight spaces, potentially slipping away from a following vehicle.

**Ram Recovery:**
: Recover from a collision without losing speed.

**Drift:**
: Execute a turn at Highway Speed or above without losing speed.

**Vehicle Swap:**
: Only useable when vehicles are 1 or fewer points of relative distance away from one another. Allows a character to move from one moving vehicle to another with an Acrobatics (Reflexes) Action Roll CR 13.

**Break Through:**
: Ram through a roadblock or barrier at an optimal angle. Removes the multiplier from the collision damage.
