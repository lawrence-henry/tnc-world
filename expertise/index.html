    Chapter 4: Particulars of Expertise¶

    Traits¶

    What a Trait Represents

    A Trait represents focused training or experience, not raw talent. As such:

    • Traits do not grant new actions unless explicitly stated.
    • Traits provide a situational bonus to Action Rolls where applicable.
    • Traits may be referenced by other rules (Expertise, Chance Actions, Equipment), but are not locked to them (if another Trait could apply, speak to your GM).
    • Traits never stack with other Traits on the same Action Roll.

    A Trait is a focused area of proficiency within its related ability. A Trait placed in a Primary Ability grants a +3 bonus when making an Action Roll related to it. This bonus is only a +2 for Secondary Ability Traits. The GM will usually let you know what Trait applies to each test, but if it isn't clear don't be afraid to ask if a Trait applies.

    Starting at Level 11, you may select a Trait you already have, to increase your bonus with it by 1. This increases Primary Ability Traits to a +4, and Secondary Ability Traits to a +3.

    Variable Traits are those that don't fit cleanly into any single category. When you take a Trait in the Variable list, you can apply it to one of your Ability Scores. You must be able to justify your use of a Trait should your choice be questionable. Be sure to discuss these with your GM to determine.

    Creating Unique Traits

    If a GM or Player can justify the creation of a Trait, feel free to do so. GM's, especially, should do this for high-profile NPCs and elite enemies.

    The primary caveat, of course, is to be sure the scope of your Trait is important enough to give you an edge, but limited in scope to keep the game fair.

    Info

    Note that Traits tagged with (Weapon Group) do not give you Weapon Group training. Weapon Group Training, however, is a prerequisite for taking one of these Traits. See #character-creation, or #equipment.

    Variable Traits¶
    Athletics Executing physical maneuvers, including climbing, running, and grappling.
    Craftsmanship Producing high-quality items. When combined with a Crafting Expertise, grants an additional Minor Advantage (+2) on the Test.
    Intimidation Influencing others through threats, posture, or sheer presence.
    Light Melee (Weapon Group) Fighting with weapons from the Light Melee group, often usable with either Might or Reflexes.
    Multitasking Managing multiple actions simultaneously. Once per encounter, gain an additional Minor Action.
    Profession Performing specialized career or trade-oriented tasks.

    Might Traits¶
    Archaic Melee (Weapon Group) Fighting with melee weapons from pre-modern eras.
    Climbing Scaling vertical surfaces and overcoming obstacles.
    Heavy Melee (Weapon Group) Fighting with large, high-impact melee weapons.
    Improvised Melee (Weapon Group) Fighting with handheld objects not designed as weapons.
    Jumping Covering distance through leaps and vaults.
    Martial Arts (Weapon Group) Fighting Unarmed, using trained techniques and specialized stances.
    Smithing Forging and shaping metal into weapons, armor, and tools.
    Strength Performing feats of raw power, such as lifting, carrying, or breaking objects.

    Reflexes Traits¶
    Acrobatics Performing gymnastic maneuvers, balancing, and tumbling.
    Archaic Ranged (Weapon Group) Fighting with ranged weapons from pre-modern eras.
    Calligraphy Writing with precision and artistic flair.
    CQB Using ranged weapons effectively in melee combat.
    Crafting Making items with precise manual skills like woodworking, sculpting, glassblowing, etc.
    Driving Directing and guiding motorized ground or naval vehicles.
    Initiative Acting quickly in tense situations.
    Lock Picking Opening locks without using keys.
    Piloting Directing and guiding an air vehicle.
    Riding Directing a mount or non-motorized wheeled vehicle.
    Sleight of Hand Using tricks of movement to manipulate objects in unsuspected ways.
    Stealth Sneaking about quietly and out of sight.
    Thrown Weapons (Weapon Group) Fighting with weapons that are most effective when thrown.

    Constitution Traits¶
    Breath Control Keeping your breathing even and steady in situations where breathing may be difficult.
    Drinking Consuming larger quantities of alcohol and avoiding the after-effects.
    Immunity Avoiding the effects of various substances like infections and diseases.
    Running Moving quickly in both short sprints and long-distance hauls.
    Stamina Enduring fatigue and privation.
    Swimming Moving through liquid and staying afloat where needed.

    Brewing Making ales, wines, and other alcohol-based concoctions.
    Computer Use Operating a personal computer, or other device.
    Cryptography Creating and deciphering codes and ciphers.
    Cultural Lore Knowing the traditions and beliefs of various cultures.
    Design Planning the creation of an item.
    Electrical Knowing the practicalities of electrical circuits.
    Engineering Knowing the practicalities of mechanical construction and building.
    Explosives (Weapon Group) Handling, constructing, dismantling, and operating weapons from the Explosives group.
    Evaluation Determining the value of goods and items.
    Healing Aiding the wounded and sick.
    Heavy-Arms (Weapon Group) Fighting with specialized heavy projectile weapons.
    Historical Lore Knowing important events and personalities from the past.
    Jury-Rigging Knowing how to build temporary items to perform specific tasks.
    Military Lore Knowing music theory, tradition, and songs.
    Natural Lore Knowing flora and fauna, and their potential uses.
    Navigation Planning and following a route from one place to another.
    Poison Lore Knowing about poisonous substances, their uses, and their preparation.
    Research Making a systematic investigation, usually using records, archives, and books.
    Religious Lore Knowing religious traditions and practices.
    Writing Expressing yourself with the written word.

    Willpower Traits¶
    Animal Handling Interacting with and caring for animals.
    Bargaining Negotiating with others and making deals.
    Courage Maintaining personal confidence and refusing to succumb to fear effects.
    Deception Lying to and tricking others using fast talk and subterfuge.
    Etiquette Knowing the social niceties of various cultures.
    Faith Deriving inner strength through spiritual or moral belief.
    Leadership Guiding, directing, and inspiring others.
    Morale Maintaining good spirits and confidence in yourself or your group.
    Performance Entertaining an audience with an artistic expertise.
    Persuasion Convincing others to agree with you.
    Self-Discipline Focusing your mental energy or controlling your impulses and emotions.

    Perception Traits¶
    Empathy Discerning the feelings, emotions, and intentions of others.
    Hearing Using your auditory sense to notice and identify things.
    Improvised Range (Weapon Group) Fighting with projectile-launching objects not normally classified as weapons.
    Long-Arms (Weapon Group) Fighting with long-barelled firearms.
    Searching Finding things that are hidden or obscured.
    Seeing Using your visual sense to notice and identify things.
    Small-Arms (Weapon Group) Fighting with small hand-held firearms.
    Smelling Using your olfactory sense to notice and identify things.
    Tracking Following footprints and other signs of passage through an area.

    Info

    The Bargaining, Leadership, and Profession Traits each multiply RP gained from monthly wages, leveling, and missions; by 2, 3, or 4, depending on Trait level and whether the trait is Primary or Secondary. The benefits from these Traits do not stack.

    Working in Multiple Disciplines¶

    Cross-Training at a Glance

    • Gain the benefits of a second Background
    • Recieve:
      • 1 Weapon Group (if available)
      • 1 Background Benefit
      • +10 RP Income
    • Can be taken twice (with expanded options at Level 11+)

    Some people career hop. Some go into dual-degree programs. There isn't a one-size-fits-all categorization of background experiences. Since the backgrounds in TNC function effectively like classes in other systems, and being trained for various applications of skill is a real-life experience, these Traits operate outside of the normal Trait rules. Like Variable Traits, these can potentially fit under multiple Abilities, and can therefore be listed under any of them.

    Instead of the normal bonus gained by taking a Trait, these options allow you to pick up an additional Background, and grant you the following from the chosen secondary Background: 1 Weapon Group Training (if available), 1 chosen Background Benefit, and their monthly RP earnings increases by +10 for leveling and monthly income purchases. If taken a second time at or beyond level 11, the PC can acquire 1 additional Background training, using the above rules, which stack with the first. Alternatively, they can gain 1 additional Background benefit from their first cross-training, and their RP earnings increase by another (for a maximum of +20). This does not affect Mission income.

    Expertises¶

    Expertise is what separates the commanders from their rank-and-file counterparts. As characters grow in their abilities, it's likely they'll specialize in mastering their disciplines. Prerequisite training required to begin training in an expertise is indicated in its block.

    Each Expertise comes in four tiers, which must be taken in order: Basic, which covers the bare minimum of training required to use an Expertise's abilities. Initiate, which indicates a moderate level of training. Advanced, which is the point at which mastery begins to truly show. And, finally, Expert, which indicates true, full mastery in a given Expertise.

    The Expertises listed are separated into categories, indicating how they would most likely be used.

    Combat¶

    Agile Defense Styles¶

    Type: Style

    Tags: Defense, Melee, Reaction, Mobility

    Prerequisites: Trained in Martial Arts, No Equipped Armor

    Defense is not optional for you. Your art is your bond. Your spirit is your guide. You seek safety in the heart of danger.

    Role: Reactive defense and counter-pressure Playstyle: Stay mobile, bait attacks, punish mistakes

    Benefits
    Basic: +2 Defense, Speed Boost
    Initiate: Improved Defense Bonuses, Improvised Cover
    Advanced: Swift-Shift (1 CP), Brutal Counter (2 CP)
    Expert: Passive Defense +5, Unique Feature - Only The Strong

    You gain +2 Defense.

    Once per round, when you are targeted by an attack, your Speed increases by 1 + ½ level (round down) yards until the start of your next turn.

    Using the Defend Minor Action while in melee combat grants you a bonus to your Defense equal to half of the Defense Bonus of one chosen opponent within your Melee Range until the start of your next turn.

    A target chosen this way becomes Improvised Cover against Ranged Attack Rolls and Area Effect attacks.

    Additionally, when you use the Defend Action, you gain a Major Advantage on your next attack roll against a target within your Melee Range. If this benefit is not used before the end of your next turn, it is lost.

    • Swift-Shift (1 CP): Use Defend as an Instinct Action.
    • Brutal Counter (2 CP): If an enemy misses their attack within your Melee Range due to your use of the Defend Action, you may add half of your Reflexes Ability and the target's last used Attack Bonus to your next Melee Damage Roll. If your attack already uses Reflexes, use your Willpower Ability for the bonus instead.
    • Defense bonus increases to +5.
    • Only The Strong: If an enemy misses you with an attack within your Melee Range due to your use of the Defend Action, that enemy takes your Unarmed or Melee Attack Damage as part of their action.

    Archery Style¶

    Type: Style Tags: Offense, Ranged Prerequisites: Trained with Archaic Ranged Weapons; Archaic Ranged Weapon Equipped

    Your arm is steady, and your aim is true. Although your weapon has been overshadowed by conventional fighting methods, that doesn't mean it can be safely overlooked.

    Role: Active offense

    Benefits
    Basic: Ignore 4 AP; Increased Range.
    Initiate: +2 Aim; Faster Reload.
    Advanced: Faster Attack
    Expert: Ignore Cover

    Your Ranged Attacks using an Archaic Ranged Weapon gain the Armor-Piercing Property, ignoring up to 4 points of a target's AP.

    Additionally, increase all Weapon Range Increments with these weapons by 5 + 1/2 level (round down) yards.

    • Focused Shot: When you use the Aim Minor Action, gain an additional +2 bonus on all Ranged Attack Rolls until the end of your turn.
    • Rapid Reload: You can reload a bowgun as a Minor Action. All other Archaic Ranged Weapons can be reloaded as a Free Action.

    • Rain of Arrows: While using an Archaic Ranged Weapon, you can treat your Ranged Attack Roll with this kind of weapon as a Minor Action.

    • Additionally, you can perform the Lightning Attack for 2 CP instead of the usual 3.

    • Masterful Aim: While using an Archaic Ranged Weapon, you can ignore up to 3/4 cover.

    Bladesman Style¶

    Type: Style Tags: Offense, Melee Prerequisites: Trained with Archaic Melee Weapons; Melee Weapon Equipped

    You have studied ancient weapons and fighting styles, finding a practical use for them in the modern era. A pillar of dedication, you stand strong in the face of danger.

    Role: Active offense

    Benefits
    Basic: +2 Defense
    Initiate: Parry (2 CP)
    Advanced: Deep Wounds (1+ CP)
    Expert: Cull The Weak (9 CP)

    As long as you wield a bladed melee weapon with a Minimum Might Requirement of 3 or less, gain a +2 bonus to your Defense. Heavier weapons only allow a +1 bonus to your Defense.

    • Perfect Parry: When you are targeted by a Melee Attack, you can roll an opposing Melee Attack Roll with a +1 bonus to the roll, instead of relying on Defense. If you succeed, you manage to push the enemy's attack away. If you roll doubles, gain a Minor Advantage (+2) on your next Melee Attack Roll. If you roll triples, you can make one immediate Melee Attack as a Non-Action.
    • Deep Wounds (1+ CP): Instead of dealing direct damage, you can impose a number of points of the Bleeding Condition equal to the amount of CP spent into this Chance Action.
    • Cull The Weak (9 CP): While your target is suffering the Bleeding Condition, you can use this unique Chance Action. Roll a Melee Attack as a Major Action, regardless of any effects that increase your attack speed. On a successful hit, you gain a cumulative +1 bonus to your Melee Attack Damage per point of Bleeding Condition currently applied to your target.

    Boxing Style¶

    Type: Style Tags: Offense, Melee Prerequisites: Trained in Martial Arts; No Weapon Equipped

    You can float, and you can sting. Any proper fighter should be good at using their fists when possible, as pugilism is the way of the strong.

    Role: Active offense

    Benefits
    Basic: +2 Damage
    Initiate: +Per Defense
    Advanced: Hard Hook (1 CP)
    Expert: Rope-A-Dope (5 CP)

    When you make an Unarmed Attack, you can spend your Minor Action to increase your Unarmed Damage by +2.

    On any round where you have not moved two yards or more, you gain half your Perception as a Bonus to your Defense.

    • Hard Hook (2+ CP): On any round where you have moved at least two yards or more, you can ignore up to 1 AP per 2 CP spent, provided by Light or Medium Armor with your Unarmed Attack.
    • Rope-A-Dope (4 CP): After you have used the Hard Hook Chance Action, until the start of your next turn, any Melee Attacks you are targeted and missed by cause your Attacker to gain Winded (T1+) Condition, the Tier of Winded increasing for every missed Melee Attack.

    CQB Style¶

    Type: Style

    Tags: Offense, Melee, Ranged

    Prerequisites: Trained in Martial Arts; Ranged Weapon Equipped

    Close quarters excite you. You've made being too close to an enemy into an artform.

    Role: Balanced Offense/Defense

    Benefits
    Basic: Activate + Aim on Initiative, Hard to Disarm
    Initiate: Riposte 2 CP, Ranged Counterattack
    Advanced: Double-Tap (2 CP)
    Expert: Moving Target (4 CP)

    You can use the Activate and Aim Minor Actions as part of your Initiative Roll. Additionally, any attempt to Disarm you is made at a Major Disadvantage (-5).

    As long as you are within CQB range of an opponent, you can use the Riposte Chance Action as an Instinct Action for 2 CP instead of the usual 4. Additionally, you can use a Ranged Attack for the Riposte counterattack instead of a Melee Attack. This allows you to use Riposte against Ranged Attacks that miss you.

    • Double-Tap (2 CP): You can make a single Ranged Attack Roll count as making 2 Ranged Attacks using the same Major Action, dealing damage with both as normal. These attacks must be aimed at the same target, and you must have at least two pieces of ammunition loaded into your weapon.
    • Moving Target (4 CP): When you are targeted by a Ranged Attack while in CQB range with another enemy, you can use your Instinct Action to impose a Major Disadvantage (-5) on the attack roll. If the attack misses, you can immediately move up to 2 yards without provoking attacks of opportunity. If the attack misses by 5 or less, it hits the enemy you were in CQB range with instead of you.

    Crusher Style¶

    Type: Style

    Tags: Offense, Melee

    Prerequisites: Might 2 or Higher, Trained in Strength, Melee Weapon Equipped

    Why wait for the tables to turn, when you can just hit them with a chair?

    Role: Aggressive Offense

    Benefits
    Basic: +4 Damage
    Initiate: Double Footwork Distance
    Advanced: Chair Breaker (1+ CP), Armor Breaker (3+ CP)
    Expert: Ability Damage

    Whenever you roll doubles on an Attack Roll with a Bludgeoning Melee Weapon, you deal an additional +4 Damage.

    For every CP spent into the Footwork Chance Action, double the distance you push your target. Any target moved more than 3 yards this way must make a Reflexes Saving Throw (CR 9 + half yards moved) or fall prone.

    • Chair Breaker (1+ CP): You may spend up to 6 CP. You deal extra damage equal to the amount of CP spent, plus your weapon's effective AP. Doing so damages your Melee Weapon. Every CP spent causes the weapon to lose one AP Threshold until it's Completely Destroyed.
    • Armor Breaker (3+ CP): Instead of dealing direct damage, you can attempt to destroy a target's armor. A successful hit forces the target to make a Reflexes Saving Throw (CR 10 + your Might). On a failure, the target's armor loses one AP Threshold per 3 CP spent, until Completely Destroyed.

    Whenever you roll doubles on an Attack Roll with a Bludgeoning Melee Weapon, you deal your Might in Damage to one of your target's Abilities. This damage persists for a number of rounds equal to your Chance Die. If you roll triples, the damage persists a number of days equal to your Chance Die.

    Iron Giant Style¶

    Type: Style

    Tags: Defense

    Prerequisites: Hardened Skin Mutation; or Light, Medium, or Heavy Armor equipped.

    Your armor is almost a second skin to you. You've trained extensively in its effective uses, and as such are not as hindered as others.

    Role: Defense

    Benefits
    Basic: 1/2 AP to Speed
    Initiate: Stand Firm as Instinct Action, Armor is 1/2 Cover.
    Advanced: Bear The Brunt (3 CP)
    Expert: Determined Protector

    You take only half of your Armor's penalty to your Speed. Additionally, you ignore your Armor's penalty to other Reflexes-based Action Rolls.

    You can use Stand Firm as an Instinct Action, instead of as a Minor Action. Additionally, your Armor counts as 1/2 Cover for you.

    • Bear The Brunt (3 CP): You can ignore Armor Piercing from all sources, including from Chance Actions, until the start of your next turn.
    • Determined Protector: If an Attack targets any character other than you, you can use your Instinct Action to interpose yourself, automatically taking the hit. Targets protected in this way benefit from you as 3/4ths Cover. The target of this ability must be within 6 yards to benefit from this action.

    Knife Fighting Style¶

    Type: Style

    Tags: Offense, Melee

    Prerequisites: Reflexes 3 or Higher, Trained in Archaic Melee or Light Melee; One-Handed Light Melee Weapon Equipped

    You laugh in the face of the idiot that brought a gun to a knife fight.

    Role: Offense

    Benefits
    Basic: +2 Damage
    Initiate: Reverse Grip
    Advanced: Stick-and-Move (1+ CP)
    Expert: Between The Plates (6 CP)

    You deal an additional +2 bonus to Damage when Attacking with a one-handed Light Melee Weapon that deals Slashing Damage.

    Reverse Grip: You can use a Minor Action to flip the Light Melee Weapon over in your hand to a reverse grip, improving your control at the cost of distance and damage. While this is active, your Melee Attack Range becomes 1 yard, and you gain a +5 bonus to Attack Rolls. Doing so imposes a -5 penalty to your Damage.

    • (Stick-and-Move 1+ CP): On a successful hit, you impose a Minor Disadvantage (-2) on your target's next attack. If used more than once, this becomes a Major Disadvantage (-5). An attack that misses you because of this ability allows you to launch a single counterattack as an Instinct Action. Instead of dealing damage, this counterattack imposes an amount of Bleeding Tiers equal to the amount of Chance Points spent into it.
    • (6 CP): When you perform a Called Shot, your attack forces the target to roll their Saving Throw against suffering a Condition, regardless of equipment or armor that would normally protect against the effects of Called Shots.

    Lone Wolf Style¶

    Type: Style

    Tags: Defense

    Prerequisites: Perception 3 or Higher, Reflexes 3 or Higher, Trained in Martial Arts

    Your opponents' final thoughts are generally about how foolish it was to surround you.

    Role: Defense

    Benefits
    Basic: Can't be outnumbered.
    Initiate: Bonus Defense, Dual Strike (2 CP)
    Advanced: Dance of the Reaper (1+ CP)
    Expert: Broken Pack

    As long as you remain outnumbered, you ignore Advantage gained on attacks targeting you due to number of enemies.

    As long as you remain outnumbered, your Defense gains a bonus equal to the number of targets whose Advantage has been ignored. Additionally, you can use the Dual Strike Chance Action for 2 CP instead of 4.

    • Dance of the Reaper(1+ CP): Your movement speed increases by 5 yards per CP spent. Make a series of Melee or Ranged attacks against a number of targets equal to CP spent, as long as you can reasonably reach them with your movement. This is made as a single Attack Action Roll, compared to each target's Defense, dealing Chance Damage on each, unless you roll triples, in which case you deal Critical Damage.
    • Broken Pack: As long as you remain outnumbered, you gain Major Advantage on a number of your Attacks equal to the number of targets whose Advantage was ignored.

    Master Key Style¶

    Type: Style

    Tags: Offense

    Prerequisites: Trained in Long-Arms

    Doors are not a barrier to you, so long as you have ammunition in your shotgun. Enemies don't fare much better.

    Role: Battlefield Control

    Benefits
    Basic: Shotguns gain Destructive property
    Initiate: All Shotguns Semi-Auto
    Advanced: Door-Kicker, Short-Stocking
    Expert: Point-Blank Master (9 CP), Rapid Breach (3 CP)

    A shotgun you wield gains the Destructive property, ignoring up to 10 AP when used against objects not being worn or carried.

    You can rack your shotgun almost as fast as you can fire it. You may treat a shotgun with the Single Shot property as though it has the Semi-Auto property, allowing it to fire multiple shots per turn.

    • Door-Kicker (1 CP): You've mastered the art of transitioning from breaching to combat. After firing a ranged weapon, you can move up to half your speed in yards. This allows you to maintain momentum when breaching doors, moving through cover, or maintaining tactical superiority.
    • Short-Stocking: When engaging a target within Short Range or less with a Long-Arms weapon, you gain a bonus to your Ranged Attack Damage equal to your Reflexes.
    • Rapid Breach (2 CP): You can fire your shotgun at an object that is not being worn or carried (such as a door or barrier), as a Free Action. This allows you to quickly move through doors or cover and immediately take your next combat action without losing time.

    MMA Style¶

    Type: Style

    Tags: Adaptive

    Prerequisites: Trained in Martial Arts

    You have trained in a variety of Martial Arts Styles, adapting what you like and discarding the rest. No opponent knows quite what to expect from you next.

    Note: Benefits from this style do not stack with their original style's benefits (for example, you can only use Basic Striking Style's benefits using MMA Style or Striking Style, but not both at the same time).

    Role: Adaptable, Offense, Defense

    Benefits
    Basic: Any Basic Style, switch 5 times per Rest.
    Initiate: Any Initiate Style, switch 2 times per Rest.
    Advanced: Any Advanced Style, switch 1 time per Rest.
    Expert: Any Expert Style, Fixed.

    Pick one Basic Tier Ability of any Expertise with the Style Tag. 5 times per Rest, as a Free Action, you can swap this ability for another Basic Tier Ability. You can only gain the benefits from one Basic Tier Ability in this way at a time.

    Pick one Initiate Tier Ability of any Expertise with the Style tag. 2 times per Rest, as a Minor Action, you can swap this ability for another Initiate Tier Ability. You can only gain the benefits from one Initiate Tier Ability in this way at a time.

    • Pick one Advanced Tier Ability of any Expertise with the Style tag. 1 time per rest, as a Major Action, you can swap this ability for another Advanced Tier Ability. You can only gain the benefits from one Advanced Tier Ability in this way at a time.
    • Pick one Expert Tier Ability of any Expertise with the Style Label. This ability can only be swapped during an extended Rest (i.e.: as a downtime activity). You can only gain the benefits from one Expert Tier Ability in this way at a time.

    Rifleman Style¶

    Type: Style

    Tags: Ranged, Offense

    Prerequisites: Trained in Long-Arms

    There are many weapons like it, but this one is yours. When all else forsakes you, your rifle is your unconditional friend.

    Role:

    Benefits
    Basic: +2 Attack with Long-Arms
    Initiate: Aim as a Free Action
    Advanced: Suppressive Fire (4 CP)
    Expert: Rapid Suppression (2 CP)

    Your familiarity with long barelled weapons grants you a +2 bonus to Attack Rolls with Long-Arms.

    Once per Round, you can use Aim as a Free Action.

    • Suppressive Fire (4 CP): You make a Ranged Attack targeting a 10 yard cone. Instead of rolling, all targets in that cone must make a Reflexes Saving Throw (CR 9 + your Perception). On a failed save, they take your weapon's damage, altered as normal by doubles or triples. On a successful save, they take no Damage. Targets benefitting from 3/4ths cover automatically succeed on this save.
    • Rapid Suppression (2 CP): As an Expert Rifleman, your precision and timing are unmatched, allowing you to perform the unique Suppressive Fire Chance Action for 2 CP instead of the usual 4. THis mastery of battlefield control lets you dictate the flow of combat. Cover no longer allows automatic success on the Saving Throw.

    Small-Arms Expert Style¶

    Type:

    Tags:

    Prerequisites: Trained in Small-Arms

    Your skill with small firearms is something to behold.

    Role:

    Benefits
    Basic: Draw and Holster as a Non-Action. Reload as an Instinct Action.
    Initiate: Aim gives +2 bonus.
    Advanced: Deadeye Shot (2 CP)
    Expert: Lethal Focus

    You can draw and holster a Small-Arms weapon as a Non-Action. Additionally, you can reload a Small-Arms Weapon as an Instinct Action.

    When you use Aim while wielding a Small-Arms Weapon, you gain a +2 bonus to your Attack Roll instead of the usual +1.

    • Deadeye Shot (2 CP): Once per Round, you can reroll a single failed Attack Roll with a Small-Arms weapon, and may use either result.
    • Lethal Focus: While using a Small-Arms Weapon, if you roll Doubles, your Damage ignores up to 4 AP provided by a target's armor or cover. If you roll triples, your Damage ignores up to 6 AP provided by a target's armor or cover.

    Stick Fighting Style¶

    Type:

    Tags:

    Prerequisites: Trained in Martial Arts, Trained in Archaic Melee, Light Melee, or Heavy Melee; Melee Weapon Equipped

    You have focused your training into lengthy or archaic weapons such as staves or spears.

    Role:

    Benefits
    Basic: Melee Range becomes 4 yards
    Initiate: Stand Firm as Instinct Action with +2 bonus
    Advanced: Heart Strike (1 CP), Sweeping Strike
    Expert: Whirling Staff (4 CP)

    Using the length of your weapon to your advantage, you treat enemies up to 4 yards away as if they were adjacent to you, so you can make Melee Attacks against them (they can't do the same unless they're also using this Expertise). If your way to that target is blocked by cover or another character, you can still attack that target using your Melee Weapon, with a penalty to the Attack Roll equal to the AP of the cover used.

    You can use the Stand Firm Chance Action as an Instinct Action, following its other rules as normal, but with a +2 bonus to your resistance against Footwork or Knock Prone. Additionally, if your weapon has the Double Property, you can now attack twice with the same Major Action.

    • Heart Strike (1 CP): You can perform the Pierce Armor Chance Action for 1 CP instead of the usual 2.
    • Sweeping Strike: You can perform the Knock Prone Chance Action as a Free Action.
    • Whirling Staff (4 CP): You move your weapon in a devastating spiral, striking down enemies with brutal efficiency. As a Major Action, you can make a single Melee Attack against all enemies considered adjacent to you. Each enemy hit must make a Constitution Saving Throw (CR 10 + your Level) or take your weapon's Chance Damage and be Dazed until the end of your next turn.

    Role:

    Benefits
    Basic: Push a target away.
    Initiate: Unarmed Attack Range -1 yard, Damage +3.
    Advanced: Whip Kick (2 CP)
    Expert: Impact Finish (9 CP)

    When you roll Doubles on an Unarmed Attack Roll, you can choose to push your targetup to 1+ your Chance Die in yards, in a straight line away from you. A target that is moved more than 3 yards in this way must make a Reflexes Saving Throw (CR 8 + yards moved) or fall Prone.

    Spend a Minor Action. For the next minute (4 rounds) of combat, your Unarmed Attack Range is decreased to 1 yard, but your Unarmed Attack Damage is increased by +3. Additionally, when you choose to Push on Doubles, as long as you have movement and attacks left available, you can follow the target and attack again.

    • Whip Kick (2 CP): Choose one of three points on your target; Low, Middle, or High. Instead of dealing Damage on a sucessful hit, your target must make a Constitution Saving Throw (CR 10 + your Might Ability). Failure causes one Tier of one of these Conditions (Half their Chance Die, rounded down):

    • Low: Shaken, Slowed, or Prone

    • Middle: Nauseated, Weakened, or Winded.

    • High: Confused, Dazed, or Enraged

    • Full Onslaught (6 CP): When a target is suffering any Condition caused by your Whip Kick Chance Action, you can deliver a devastating follow-up attack. Make an additional Unarmed Attack Roll as an Instinct Action. If the attack hits, the target takes an additional +1 Damage per Condition inflicted by Whip Kick (max. +3). The target must then make a Saving Throw (CR 11 + your Chance Die), determined by your last successful Whip Kick Height:

    • Low: Might Save

    • Middle: Constitution Save

    • High: Willpower Save

    On a failed Save, the target becomes Helpless for a number of rounds equal to your Chance Die.

    Type:

    Tags:

    Prerequisites: Trained in Strength

    Role:

    Benefits
    Basic: +2 bonus to Grapples
    Initiate: Free Action Grapple after Unarmed Hit

    Expert: Iron Grip (4 CP), Throw Combination (6+ CP), Hurricane Throw (6 CP)

    When you successfully hit a target with an Unarmed Attack, you can make an immediate Grapple attempt as a Free Action.

Your target lands Prone a number of yards away equal to your Chance Die. You can keep hold of the Grapple and refuse the throw distance by spending an additional +4 CP.
    • Suplex (1+ CP): You can perform the Knock Prone Chance Action for 1 CP instead of the usual 2. You can keep hold of the Grapple by spending an additional +1 CP.
    • Iron Grip (4 CP): While you are in control of a Grapple, you can spend your Instinct Action to impose a Major Disadvantage (-5) to all of your held target's Might or Reflexes-related Action Rolls until they break free, or the start of your next turn, whichever comes first.
    • Throw Combination (6+ CP): You slam your target on the ground, or into an object, multiple times. This attack deals your Grapple Strike Damage once for every 3 CP spent into it. If slammed into an object, add an amount of Damage equal to the AP value of the object's material.
    • Hurricane Throw (6 CP): Make a Grapple Attempt. You jump onto your target's shoulders and flip backwards, throwing them in the process. This attack deals twice your Reflexes in Bludgeoning Damage. If the target strikes an object, this Chance Action deals an additional amount of Damage equal to the AP value of the object's material.
